### Details
In recent epochs, an extremely high amount of execution fee reserves has been burned due to the following activities: 
- Targeted spamming of unprotected procedures “addLiquidity” and “removeLiquidity”  
- Drastic increase of execution fees on the Computor side

The amount burned was so high that the continued operation of the contract can only be guaranteed for a few more weeks at most.
The risk and criticality for QSWAP have been recognized by the Core Team and the Computors. As a gesture of goodwill, the charging of execution fees was temporarily paused for a short period.
However, every affected Smart Contract is required to take action and close its vulnerabilities.  

The community is very grateful for this extreme test and the valuable learning effect. 
Unfortunately, QSWAP has no clear owner and no liquidity for such short-term smart contract adjustments.  
Thanks to the engagement of some community members, this originally abandoned contract has become one of the successful ones. 
We still have many plans ahead. But today we are asking for short-term and important support in hardening the Smart Contract.

We have quickly found Smart Contract developers who are ready to implement all the necessary protective measures.

What will be implemented:
- All procedures that don’t currently charge any fees will be expanded to include additional fees. Examples are the two procedures “addLiquidity” and “removeLiquidity”.  
- Fees will be adjusted for all procedures so that attacks or abuse are no longer financially worthwhile.  
- All fees can be queried via the “Fees” function.
- Reducing the size of the state.

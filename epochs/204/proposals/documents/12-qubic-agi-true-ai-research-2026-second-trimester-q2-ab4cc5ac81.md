## Introduction

The Qubic Open Science Team (Dr. José Sanchez & David Vivancos) has been an integral part of Qubic since 2024, with the aim of setting the scientific grounding for making Qubic a key relevant player in the AGI space, following the vision outlined by CFB since the beginning, with a stronger needed push in 2026.

This proposal is a continuation towards that goal of following and advancing the work from the first 2026 trimester.

---

## 2026 Q1 Milestones

2026 has started with several key advances achieved:

1. **Developing and updating the Neuraxon Architecture**, including the release of the Neuraxon 2.0 blueprint in code, scientific paper, and working demos. *(Reference: [Neuraxon V2.0: A New Neural Growth Computation Blueprint](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/400868863_Neuraxon_V20_A_New_Neural_Growth_Computation_Blueprint))*
2. **Developing multiple releases** of Neuraxon Game Of Life, Research Version, and Lite version to explore in a real competitive computational simulation how the architecture can become the foundation of future digital brains.
3. **Creating several datasets** based on the Game Of Life releases, from snapshots to huge terabyte-scale time series.
4. **Creating the first physical proof** that the Neuraxon architecture can be used in real life as a “brain” for controlling a Sphero Mini robot.
5. **Documenting the scientific grounding** with 2 additional academic papers (besides Neuraxon 2.0) to prove empirically parts of the architecture, and presenting them to peer review to international tier 1 & 2 venues. This achieved the milestone of one paper (exploring the ternary advantage) being accepted for publication and presentation at AMLDS in Osaka, Japan on July 21st to 23rd *(a separate proposal is issued in parallel to cover related expenses)*.
6. **Starting the new Neuraxon Academy** with periodic articles and interactive demos to explain the details of the architecture to all audiences, alongside key new papers in the AI space related to what Qubic is doing.
7. **Updating periodically** the digital open repositories at GitHub & HuggingFace.
8. **Finding the parts** of the Neuraxon Aigarth architecture that are more relevant and suited for large scale search using the Qubic network compute more optimally, with pseudo code to be ported there *(a separate proposal will be issued in April for the development in sync with CFB and core dev)*.

# Community Reflection and Accountability

Well, it’s safe to say the year is coming to an end — and I guess this is a good time to reflect. It’s been one hell of a ride, huh? Unfortunately, not in a good way.

Like any complex project, Qubic has had its ups and downs, tough moments, and high hopes. Normally, after the storm, the rain stops, and the dark clouds fade away… but not this time. Not with Qubic.

Yes, some progress has been made — but the big breakthrough, the “promised” upside, and the price action we were told to expect never really came. Were we hopeful that this was finally our moment to shine?  
**Absolutely.**  
Was it the first time we felt that way?  
**No.**  
And probably not the last either.

Now, before anyone jumps to conclusions — I’m not here to spread hate, fear, FUD, or cause drama. I’m not here to attack or blame anyone. I’m simply here to ask a few honest questions that many of us are quietly thinking:

- Are those who manage and represent Qubic genuinely prioritizing the long-term success of the project over personal or short-term interests?  
- Are they really what Qubic needs right now?  
- Do their high salaries truly reflect the value and impact of their work?  

But my main question is for you, Computors:  
**Do you agree that the people working for Qubic — representing it, speaking for it, and guiding its growth — are truly competent and acting in Qubic’s best interest?**  
And if not… should they consider stepping aside for the sake of the project’s future?

I’m not going to name anyone — real names or nicknames. This isn’t personal.  
That’s about it for now.

I apologize if this proposal breaks any rules, and just to be clear — I’m not asking for any funds.  
Let’s be honest: the Qubic Discord isn’t exactly the friendliest place for hard questions anyway.

Anyway, these are just thoughts that have been sitting in my head for a while. Maybe they’ll be ignored, maybe they’ll spark a conversation — either way, I said what I needed to say. If nothing else, I hope it reminds everyone why we came here in the first place: because we believed Qubic could be something greater.

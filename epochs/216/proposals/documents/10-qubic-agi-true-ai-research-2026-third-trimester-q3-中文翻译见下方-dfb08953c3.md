## Introduction

The Qubic Open Science Team (Dr. José Sanchez & David Vivancos) has been an integral part of Qubic since 2024, with the aim of setting the scientific grounding for making Qubic a key relevant player in the AGI space, following the vision outlined by CFB since the beginning, with a stronger needed push in 2026.

This proposal is a continuation towards that goal of following and advancing the work from the first 2026, 2 trimesters Q1 & Q2.

## 2026 Q2 Milestones

Following 2026 Q1, Q2 continued with several key advances achieved:

1. Developing and updating the Neuraxon Architecture, including the release of the Multi Neuraxon 2.0 blueprint in [code](https://github.com/DavidVivancos/Neuraxon), scientific [papers](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/400868863_Neuraxon_V20_A_New_Neural_Growth_Computation_Blueprint), and working [demos](https://huggingface.co/spaces/DavidVivancos/MultiNeuraxon2).

2. Development of 3 new Scientific Papers:

   * **Multi Neuraxon** — Presented to [AGI 2026](https://agi-conference.org/) and accepted.
   * **Neuraxon Game of Life** — Presented to [Alife 2026](https://2026.alife.org/), waiting for reviews.
   * **G factor of Intelligence in Neuraxon** — Presented to [Neurips 2026](https://neurips.cc/), waiting for reviews.

3. Developing multiple releases of Neuraxon Game Of Life (GoL), Research Version, and Lite version up to version 5.0, including up to 200 internal releases, to explore in a real competitive computational simulation how the architecture can become the foundation of future digital brains.

4. Developing Neuraxon Live 24/7 persistent server/client Neuraxon Game Of Life based on GoL 5: [https://nxon.online](https://nxon.online)
   Also Open Source.

5. Creating several datasets based on the Game Of Life releases, including GoL 5.0 NAS (Neural Architecture Search) for the exploration of brain models.

6. Creating the first physical proof that the Neuraxon architecture can be used in real life as a “brain” for controlling a Sphero Mini robot.

7. Documenting the scientific grounding with the 3 additional academic papers mentioned above and presenting them to peer review to international tier 1 & 2 venues. This achieved the milestone of 2 new papers accepted:

   * Neuraxon 2.0
   * Multi Neuraxon

8. Neuraxon 2.0 paper was accepted at ICMLT and was presented in person in Berlin in May 2026, at the 11th conference on machine learning technologies, including the award of the best paper presentation of the evening.

9. Multi Neuraxon paper was accepted to present at AGI 2026, the 19th conference and main one on AGI research worldwide. A separate proposal is issued in parallel to cover related expenses.

10. Research in Aigarth & Neuraxon hybridization and brain models, including:

    * Neuraxon Tissue Specialization
    * Criticality monitoring
    * Meta modulation
    * Reinforcement Learning

11. Exploring the ternary Qubic Research advantage, being accepted for publication and presentation at AMLDS in Osaka, Japan on July 21st to 23rd.

12. Working on the scientific grounding for the upcoming Neuraxon 3.0 Candidate.

13. Continuing the new Neuraxon Academy with periodic articles and interactive demos to explain the details of the architecture to all audiences, alongside key new papers in the AI space related to what Qubic is doing.

14. Updating periodically the digital open repositories at GitHub & HuggingFace.

15. Developed the Python code to teach and mix Neuraxon Aigarth in the Qubic Network, integrated in the ant-hill state in sync with CFB. Integration and code porting to Qiner is pending for exploration with coredev with CyberPC and, if and when needed, a separate proposal will be issued. This wasn’t issued in Q2 due to other network priorities like the Doge works, network stabilization, and initial Aigarth anthill.

16. Developed Open Source CUDA Kernels and libraries to execute/teach Multi Neuraxon on Nvidia devices, opening the door to up to 5 million developers, and widely used in the AI research community. This was not included in Q2 proposal but was delivered.

17. Building several demos for the community to communicate the science, including NXSound, which was integrated also starting in GoL 5 to provide protolanguage and the upcoming Neuraxon-Kaleido visual works.

18. NIA releases — interesting for learning more about Qubic, Aigarth, and Nxon Science:

    * **NIA Volume 1: [Why Intelligence Is Not Computed in Steps, but in Time](https://qubic.org/blog-detail/neuraxon-time-why-intelligence-is-not-computed-in-steps-but-in-time)**
      Explores why biological intelligence operates in continuous time rather than discrete computational steps like traditional LLMs.
    * **NIA Volume 2: [Ternary Dynamics as a Model of Living Intelligence](https://qubic.org/blog-detail/beyond-binary-ternary-dynamics-as-a-model-of-living-intelligence)**
      Explains ternary dynamics and why three-state logic — excitatory, neutral, inhibitory — matters for modeling living systems.
    * **NIA Volume 3: [Neuromodulation and Brain-Inspired AI](https://qubic.org/blog-detail/neuromodulation-brain-inspired-ai-neuraxon-intelligence-academy-vol-3)**
      Covers neuromodulation and how the brain's chemical signaling — dopamine, serotonin, acetylcholine, norepinephrine — inspires Neuraxon's architecture.
    * **NIA Volume 4: [Neural Networks in AI and Neuroscience](https://qubic.org/blog-detail/neural-networks-biological-artificial-neuraxon-intelligence)**
      A deep comparison of biological neural networks, artificial neural networks, and Neuraxon's third-path approach.
    * **NIA Volume 5: [Astrocytes and Brain-Inspired AI](https://qubic.org/blog-detail/astrocytes-neuraxon-intelligence-academy-vol-5)**
      How astrocytic gating transforms neural network plasticity through the AGMP framework in Neuraxon.
    * **NIA Volume 6: [Conscious Machines vs Intelligent Organisms: AI Consciousness Explained](https://qubic.org/blog-detail/conscious-machines-intelligent-organisms-ai-consciousness-neuraxon-vol-6)**
      Explores AI consciousness through the lens of Global Workspace Theory, Integrated Information Theory, and predictive coding.
    * **NIA Volume 7: [Conway's Game of Life, Artificial Life, and Digital Ecosystems](https://qubic.org/blog-detail/conways-game-of-life-artificial-life-digital-ecosystems-qubic-neuraxon-nia-vol-7)**
      How emergent complexity and self-organized criticality move from simulators to decentralized AI infrastructure.
    * **NIA Volume 8: [Brain Criticality and the Branching Ratio in Neural and Artificial Networks](https://qubic.org/blog-detail/brain-criticality-branching-ratio-neural-artificial-networks-neuraxon-nia-vol-8)**
      Why a branching ratio near 1 and self-organized criticality are bioinspired design principles in Neuraxon.
    * **NIA Volume 9: [The g Factor in Artificial Life](https://qubic.org/blog-detail/g-factor-general-intelligence-artificial-life-neuraxon-nia-vol-9)**

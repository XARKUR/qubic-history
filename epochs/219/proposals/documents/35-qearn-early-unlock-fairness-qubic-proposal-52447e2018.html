FULL PROPOSAL
PROPOSAL

Align QEarn with its 52-epoch purpose by reserving bonus rewards for lockers who complete the intended lock period
It leaves the QEarn computor allocation untouched, the issue is the early-unlock distribution logic, not the size of QEarn funding

The proposed behavior is:

  • The change applies only to new locks from activation epoch 221
  • Locks made before activation keep their original terms
  • Unlocking in the same epoch as the lock is blocked
  • Early unlockers receive principal only
  • Early unlockers receive no bonus
  • Early unlock does not burn the forfeited bonus slice
  • The forfeited bonus remains in the round for users who stay locked until maturity
  • Any early unlock after the original lock epoch exits the full position for that lock epoch
VOTING OPTIONS
OPTION 0 Keep QEarn unchangedEarly unlockers can still take part of the bonus pool and burn another part before 52-epoch maturity
OPTION 1 THIS PROPOSALApprove the early-unlock alignment fixProspective activation, no same-epoch unlock, principal-only early unlock, no early bonus, no early burn, and full exit after the lock epoch
COMPUTOR RATIONALE

Computors submit this proposal because current QEarn behavior conflicts with the intent of computor donations

Those donations are meant to reward the loyalty of users who lock for the full 52-epoch period, not to subsidize a short-term strategy that recovers principal early while still draining the donated bonus pool
Fixing early unlocks keeps that original intent intact

If the behavior stays unchanged, computors may have to cut QEarn donations to stop funding this extraction path, shrinking the reward pool for loyal users, the exact outcome this proposal prevents

PROBLEM

QEarn currently lets short-term lockers unlock before 52 epochs and still take part of the reward bonus
Part of the impacted bonus is paid early, part is burned, and only the remaining part boosts users who stay

The boost mechanism exists and is counted in this document
The issue is that computor-donated bonus is not fully reserved for the completed 52-epoch commitment

BOOST + BURN CLARIFICATION

This proposal does not ignore the current boost mechanism
In the 2026 impacted slice, about 40.2% stayed as boost, while 21.0% was paid to early unlockers and 38.8% was burned before maturity

The proposed rule is simpler: if a lock does not complete 52 epochs, its bonus share is not paid early and is not burned inside QEarn, it stays reserved for completed locks

PROPOSED CONTRACT CHANGE
Early-unlock behavior Now After patch
Existing locks before activationcurrent termsunchanged
Unlock during the lock epochallowedblocked
Reward to the unlockerpartialnone
Bonus burnedpartialnone
Unlock after the lock epochpartialfull exit

Why full exit: otherwise a large locker could unlock most of a position, leave a token amount behind, and later recapture the preserved, now-boosted bonus through the remnant

Why same-epoch unlock is blocked: otherwise a locker can enter with a large amount, affect the visible round yield, then unlock before the epoch closes

Scope: src/contracts/Qearn.h and test/contract_qearn.cpp
The tests prove prospective activation, legacy locks unchanged, same-epoch unlock blocked, no early bonus or burn, full exit after the lock epoch, and full bonus preserved for completed locks

EXPECTED OUTCOME
  • Legacy terms stay: users who locked before activation keep the rules they entered under
  • Liquidity stays after the lock epoch: new early unlocks still return principal, but no longer pay bonus
  • No leakage: new early unlocks no longer earn or burn bonus, and any post-epoch unlock is a full exit
  • Cleaner APY behavior: new same-epoch unlocks cannot be used to distort a round before it closes
  • Loyalty pays: 52-epoch lockers receive the bonus forfeited by early exits

In one line: bonus rewards belong to completed 52-epoch locks

QEARN V2 DISCUSSION

A larger QEarn V2 can be discussed separately after this patch
That discussion could include explicit 3-month, 6-month, and 12-month products with separate reward pots, target yields, and burn rules

That should not be hidden inside the current early-unlock table
The current proposal is a narrow fix for the existing 52-epoch QEarn product

DONATION CHECKPOINT

This proposal leaves the QEarn donation unchanged for now

After a few epochs under the patched rules, computors can review whether the 12.25% allocation is still the right level

  • if yield becomes too high, donation can be reduced and the difference redirected to burn or another agreed purpose
  • if yield is healthy, QEarn gets a chance to prove the simpler 52-epoch model

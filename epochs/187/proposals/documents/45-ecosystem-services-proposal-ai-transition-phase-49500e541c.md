## Self-Reflection

The Ecosystem team was created around one year and a half ago to act as a sort of **venture builder**, orchestrating to build tech capabilities around the core by and for the community, aiming to increase Qubic adoption and awareness from a decentralized standpoint.  

Since then, we can clearly identify Ecosystem team invested their resources in 3 main areas:

### 1. AI Research – Scientific Team

Qubic's main value proposition is decentralized AGI, specifically *Aigarth*, which at the very beginning only CfB and his team was working on it.  

It was decided, as a strategic investment, to include two AI researchers (**David Vivancos** and **Dr. José Sánchez**) to work in parallel challenging CfB’s approach, researching how AI could grow from a biological perspective and also covering the communication part (events, publications, etc.), where Daniel also joined to bring a more partnership/business-oriented view.

This area has proven to add consistent value to Qubic, acting as a differentiator factor from other protocols and fully aligned with Qubic ethos (AGI).  

Also, although not only related to this area, the whole Ecosystem team worked on the **publication of the whitepaper**, one of the most impactful initiatives in Qubic’s history.

Although current Scientific team performance has been good (and the investment very limited), we are reaching a point where Qubic’s narrative starts to dilute, so our bet must be stronger to position Qubic as the **leading AI protocol**.  

The last [research paper](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/397331336_Neuraxon) includes what we consider a key to success: a new open-source approach with a call to more scientists to join → [Neuraxon](https://github.com/DavidVivancos/Neuraxon).

---

### 2. Partnerships

As an engine to attract investors, subject matter experts, and professional development companies, the Ecosystem team (led by **Alber** and **Daniel**) took the assignment of building **strategic partnerships** (i.e. low-capital investment and/or favorable terms).

On one hand, Ecosystem managed to bring early adopters teams such as **Vottun** or **Avicenne Studio**, who bet on Qubic and decided to build projects.  

Also, the team managed to organise low-budget high-impact events like **MadHack**, establish connections with universities (La Latina, IE, among others), and scientific/research teams, and share Qubic’s vision in many relevant events in **Latam, EU, and Asia**.  

Although all these connections helped position Qubic and create awareness, there was minimal to no impact in price.

On the other hand, **listings** are the most demanding initiative.  
Dealing with exchanges being a small cap with very limited budget, low traction, and high decentralization is a huge challenge.  

The Ecosystem team (Alber leading all negotiations) managed to list Qubic in new exchanges such as **LBank** and **BingX** at a very good price, integrate with a **hardwallet provider**, and other partners that facilitate interoperability.  

However, given the current market sentiment and Qubic’s position, **Tier 1 listings** remain hard to achieve.

All in all, the most valuable part of this area is the ability to establish **meaningful connections and communicate Qubic’s vision and strategy** at a very detailed level while being extremely efficient (< 2 FTEs).

---

### 3. Grants & Incubation

When the Ecosystem team was created, to develop on Qubic was a completely different story from today. There were no tools, documentation was very poor and even we lacked a testnet environment. Jointly with all workgroups, specially QCT, 2 funding mechanism were set to address this situation:

- **Grants**, aiming to fund development tools and initiatives that ease the learning curve for developing on Qubic (SDKs, libraries, etc)

- **Incubation**, aiming to fund businesses built on Qubic (bridges, launchpads, lending protocols, etc)

However, this area has been the most resource and capital intensive by far. Not only we would be investing (once pending projects finalise) +600k$ (Grants&Incubation combined) but also we dedicated +4 FTEs to operate this (Lattio, MrRose, Lwen, Zgirt, former employees and other providers)

Looking back to the projects we funded, we can determine

- **Qubic now has a development environment suitable for new joiners**. They have the basic tools and documentation to be independent. No major investment is required.

- Most relevant projects (Qswap, Nosotromo, Vottun) required a huge development dedication from the Ecosystem team (fully Nostromo SC development, several CLI developments, etc), proving only experienced (Qubic) developers can address such projects.

- There are minimum services that cannot be cut regardless how decentralized we are, such as minimal PMO, QA and support development.

- Around **20%** of the investment has been dedicated to community projects that had **no impact in adoption**

- Around **25%** of the investment has been dedicated to projects that make **no big difference** if compared to other protocols

- Only 30% of the investment is being dedicated to projects that will bring liquidity, interoperability and act as the pillars/rails for our AGI strategy

- Community decisions are arbitrary when it comes to long-term, plus community engagement is low

- **RFPs are a good mechanism** to determine project boundaries and limit risks
